About Me

Hello! As an introduction, my name is Lewis and I am a Third Year Software Engineering student at McMaster University. Currently, I am searching for an exciting 4 month co-op position for Summer 2020 in a dynamic environment in which I can thrive. I am particularly interested in positions that will allow me to solve complex problems in a team of personable and bright peers. I bring a passionate and deliberate approach when writing software.

Since January 2019, I have worked part-time during the Winter and Fall semesters, and full-time during the Summer semester as a Web Application Developer at the McMaster Steel Research Centre. During my time in this position I have added value to the Centre in many ways. Primarily, I have hand-coded a full front and back-end web application that integrates a steel hot-rolling process model in the Centre's website. I have improved security by updating the password hash algorithm the system uses to PHP standard, bcrypt. I also improved efficiency by creating a tailor-made Firefox extension that automated submitting the Centre's credit card purchase reconciliations with the Materials Engineering Department. Through these experiences, I have a broad understanding of web development and have proven to be a driven self-learner.

I am, however, more than just a software enthusiast! I have many passions outside my work and school. I am an avid advocate for financial literacy and this has translated to a passion for investing. I highly enjoy watching the markets and following global news to better understand the world at large. My love of problem solving and the outdoors has translated perfectly to bouldering and climbing, where I quickly became addicted to the social, fitness and mental conditioning benefits. Since I was a toddler I have enjoyed many sports, but, consistent with the university student stereotype, I most obsessed with Ultimate frisbee at the moment.
